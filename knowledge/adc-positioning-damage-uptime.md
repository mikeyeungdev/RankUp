# ADC Positioning And Damage Uptime

Use this note when the transcript discusses ADC, bot carry, marksman, farming, spacing, damage uptime, item spikes, front-to-back fights, peel, threat tracking, or survival in fights.

Core coaching model:
- ADC impact comes from consistent farm, item timing, safe spacing, and staying alive long enough to deal repeated damage.
- Damage uptime is not only aggression. It means standing where the player can hit available targets without walking into lethal threat.
- Threat tracking matters because one missed engage tool, flank, flash, or assassin angle can erase the ADC's output.
- Front-to-back fights are usually won by hitting the safest high-value target while preserving spacing and cooldowns.
- Bot lane review should separate lane farming/trading from teamfight positioning. A player can lane well and still lose fights through spacing errors.

Review questions:
- Was the ADC farming toward a clear item spike?
- Did the player know which enemy tools could kill or force them out?
- Was the player hitting safely, over-chasing, or standing too far back to contribute?
- Did support/team setup give the ADC space, and did the ADC use it correctly?

Training goals:
- Before each fight, name the top two enemy threats that can reach the ADC.
- Review each death and label it as spacing, cooldown tracking, flank, greed, or no peel.
- Track farm at 10, 15, and 20 minutes alongside major item timings.
- In fight review, mark when the ADC could safely hit and when they needed to reposition.
