# Positioning And Teamfighting

Research basis:
- Hyse role focus areas: https://hyse.gg/
- MMRadar macro guide: https://mmradar.gg/blog/league-of-legends-macro-guide
- Riot role and teamfight basics: https://www.leagueoflegends.com/en-us/how-to-play/

Use this note when the transcript discusses spacing, target selection, engage, peel, flanks, carries, front-to-back fights, or teamfight execution.

Core coaching model:
- Teamfight positioning depends on champion role, cooldowns, enemy threats, summoner spells, vision, and objective terrain.
- A player should know their job before the fight: engage, peel, follow-up, zone, flank, front-to-back damage, objective secure, or disengage.
- Good positioning usually protects the champion's job while threatening the enemy's win condition.
- Bad positioning often shows up as entering before teammates, standing without vision, chasing past threat ranges, or using key cooldowns without a target purpose.
- Fight review should separate setup from execution: the player may lose because of pre-fight vision and wave state, not only mechanics.

Review questions:
- What was the player's job in this fight?
- Did the player enter before or after the correct cooldowns were used?
- Was the player threatening a priority target or protecting a priority ally?
- Did terrain and vision make the position safe or dangerous?

Training goals:
- Before each fight, write a one-sentence job for the champion.
- Review deaths and label them as setup, spacing, cooldown, target, or chase errors.
- Practice pausing before major fights to identify enemy engage tools and allied follow-up.
