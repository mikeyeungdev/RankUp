# Trading, Cooldowns, And Lane Combat

Use this note when the transcript discusses trading, spacing, level timers, cooldowns, summoner spells, all-ins, poking, minion damage, or matchup windows.

Core coaching model:
- Good trading is usually built around cooldown advantage, level advantage, wave position, minion count, and jungle threat.
- Trading while standing in a large enemy wave can make a winning matchup lose.
- Level 2, level 3, and level 6 timers are common windows where students either create pressure or get punished.
- Summoner spells and key ultimates create temporary rules. If Flash, Teleport, Ignite, Cleanse, Exhaust, or ultimate is down, the correct fight changes.
- A student should learn the difference between poke, short trade, extended trade, all-in, zone, and disengage.

Review questions:
- What cooldown or level advantage made this trade good or bad?
- Did the player fight inside a favorable or unfavorable minion wave?
- Was the trade taken for a purpose: crash, deny CS, setup gank, force recall, or kill threat?
- Did the player respect enemy summoner spells and ultimate timers?

Training goals:
- For the first six minutes, mark every bad trade and identify wave, cooldown, level, or jungle as the cause.
- Before trading, check the enemy key ability and your own escape tool.
- Track one matchup-specific trade rule across three games.
