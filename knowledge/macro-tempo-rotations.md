# Macro, Tempo, And Rotations

Research basis:
- MMRadar macro guide: https://mmradar.gg/blog/league-of-legends-macro-guide
- Z League macro concepts: https://www.zleague.gg/theportal/core-macro-concepts-in-league-of-legends/
- BuildZCrank macro guide: https://buildzcrank.com/en/blog/macro-guide-league-of-legends-2026/

Use this note when the transcript discusses map movement, recalls, rotations, side lanes, lane priority, or when to group.

Core coaching model:
- Macro is map-based decision-making: where to be, when to move, and what the next play should create.
- Tempo is the ability to act first because of better wave state, recall timing, health, items, or position.
- Good rotations usually start from a pushed wave, completed recall, visible enemy position, or objective timer.
- Bad rotations often abandon farm, arrive late, walk through dark areas, or group without a clear purpose.
- Lane priority matters because the player who can move first can protect vision, contest river, help jungle, or start an objective.

Review questions:
- Did this movement happen after pushing the wave or before collecting needed resources?
- Who could arrive first to the next fight or objective?
- Did the recall timing prepare the next play or make the player late?
- Was the player answering pressure, creating pressure, or wandering?

Training goals:
- Before rotating, check wave state, health, item gold, objective timer, and enemy visibility.
- Review three moments per VOD where the player moved and label each as early, on time, or late.
- Build a reset habit: crash wave, recall, buy, then arrive early to the next objective or lane assignment.
