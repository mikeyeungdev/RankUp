# VOD Review And Training Plan Framework

Research basis:
- Hyse coaching workflow: https://hyse.gg/
- Macro improvement themes from MMRadar and Z League:
  - https://mmradar.gg/blog/league-of-legends-macro-guide
  - https://www.zleague.gg/theportal/core-macro-concepts-in-league-of-legends/

Use this note to organize coaching transcripts into student-facing review notes.

Core coaching model:
- A good review separates observation, evidence, diagnosis, and practice.
- The transcript should provide the evidence. The knowledge base should only help choose labels and drills.
- Avoid overwhelming the student. Pick a small number of high-leverage patterns instead of summarizing every sentence.
- The best training plan turns recurring issues into repeatable habits with a review checkpoint.
- Prefer "when X happens, do Y" rules over vague advice.
- A professional review should rank issues by leverage, not by how often the coach mentions them. One death before Baron can matter more than five small CS misses.
- Separate primary causes from symptoms. A bad fight may be caused by late reset or poor wave setup, not the visible mechanical mistake.

Output priorities:
- Summary: one short paragraph explaining the main lesson of the VOD.
- Focus areas: three to six patterns that the transcript clearly supports.
- Training goals: concrete actions the student can practice in the next block of games.
- Drills: only include drills for skills the coach actually discussed.
- Key moments: timestamped transcript moments that prove the focus areas.
- Priority ranking: identify the one or two changes that would create the highest improvement for the student.
- Practice cadence: suggest a short block such as three games, one review checkpoint, and one measurable habit.

Review questions:
- Is each note backed by an exact transcript quote?
- Is the fix specific enough to practice in game?
- Does the plan focus on patterns instead of isolated one-off mistakes?
- Can a coach share this plan without re-explaining the entire VOD?
- Does the plan avoid adding concepts that the coach never discussed?
- Would the student know exactly what to do differently in the next match?
