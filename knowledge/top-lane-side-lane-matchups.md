# Top Lane Side Lane And Matchups

Use this note when the transcript discusses top lane, side lane, matchup discipline, Teleport, split pushing, freezing, wave control, trading, bruisers, tanks, or when to group.

Core coaching model:
- Top lane often turns small matchup edges into wave control, plate pressure, side-lane pressure, or Teleport impact.
- Matchup discipline matters because many top-lane mistakes come from fighting before the wave, cooldowns, level, or item timing supports the trade.
- Side-lane pressure is valuable only when it creates a real map question: draw pressure, take tower, threaten flank, force objective trade, or create time for teammates.
- Grouping is strongest when the player has pushed the side wave first or when their champion's teamfight job is more valuable than side-lane pressure.
- Teleport should connect lane state to map impact. A late or random Teleport can lose both the side wave and the fight.

Review questions:
- Did the player understand the matchup's safe and punish windows?
- Was the wave state helping the trade, recall, freeze, or crash?
- Did the player push side lane before grouping or teleporting?
- Was the correct job split push, absorb pressure, flank, frontline, or peel?

Training goals:
- Review the first four waves and label the intended wave state.
- Before trading, name the enemy cooldown or wave state that makes the trade good.
- In mid game, push side wave before grouping unless the transcript gives a reason not to.
- Review each Teleport and label it as lane-saving, fight-winning, late, or unnecessary.
