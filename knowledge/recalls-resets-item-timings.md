# Recalls, Resets, And Item Timings

Use this note when the transcript discusses recall timing, base timing, shopping, item spikes, tempo, reset, crash, death timers, or arriving late.

Core coaching model:
- A recall is a macro decision, not downtime. Good recalls turn gold into strength without giving up unnecessary waves or objectives.
- The cleanest reset usually follows a crashed wave, completed jungle quadrant, won fight, or objective setup window.
- Bad recalls often cause lost waves, late objective arrival, weak vision setup, or fighting with unspent gold.
- Item spikes matter because the player can be correct to avoid a fight before buying and correct to force after buying.
- A death before a major timer often functions like a bad recall with a much higher cost.

Review questions:
- Did the player recall with a wave state that protected their lane?
- Was gold spent before the next fight or objective?
- Did the player reset early enough to place vision and take position?
- Was staying on the map greed, pressure, or correct tempo?

Training goals:
- Track every recall as good, late, greedy, forced, or unnecessary.
- Before major objectives, reset early enough to buy, ward, and arrive first.
- Review one game and mark every fight taken with meaningful unspent gold.
