# Objective Control

Research basis:
- Riot Games objective basics: https://www.leagueoflegends.com/en-us/how-to-play/
- Objective control guide: https://www.cheatspulse.com/guides/league-of-legends/objective-control
- BuildZCrank objective timing guide: https://buildzcrank.com/en/blog/macro-guide-league-of-legends-2026/

Use this note when the transcript discusses dragons, Rift Herald, Voidgrubs, Baron, Elder, towers, setup timing, or objective fights.

Core coaching model:
- Objectives are how temporary advantages become durable map advantages.
- Objective setup starts before the monster spawns. The setup usually includes recall timing, wave push, vision placement, vision denial, and lane assignments.
- Arriving late to an objective usually means face-checking, losing river control, or giving up position.
- Starting an objective without lane priority or enemy information can turn an advantage into a coin flip.
- The best objective call often depends on nearby waves, health bars, Smite status, item completions, and enemy death timers.

Review questions:
- Did the team prepare vision and waves before the objective spawned?
- Did the player reset early enough to buy and return?
- Was the objective started because the enemy could not contest, or was it forced?
- If the objective was skipped, what higher-value play replaced it?

Training goals:
- At 90, 60, and 30 seconds before a major objective, check reset, wave, vision, and grouping status.
- Review each objective fight and tag the failure as late reset, no wave priority, no vision, poor position, or bad target choice.
- Practice converting won fights into the nearest durable objective instead of defaulting to recall.
