# Jungle Pathing And Tracking

Use this note when the transcript discusses jungle clears, pathing, camps, ganks, invades, tracking, crab, countergank, lane priority, or jungle matchup.

Core coaching model:
- Jungle pathing should serve a lane plan, matchup plan, or objective timer.
- Tracking the enemy jungler starts from likely starting camp, lane leash information, camp respawn timing, visible CS, gank path, and objective timers.
- Ganks are stronger when they match wave state, lane crowd control, enemy summoner spell status, and allied follow-up.
- Invades require information and lane priority. A strong duelist can still throw the game by invading without nearby lanes able to move.
- Jungle tempo is often lost through inefficient pathing, low-value ganks, late recalls, or starting objectives without lane control.

Review questions:
- Why did the jungler path toward this side of the map?
- Did the player know where the enemy jungler was likely to be?
- Was this gank supported by wave state and allied crowd control?
- Did the invade or objective have lane priority behind it?

Training goals:
- At 3:00, predict enemy jungle location using start side, lane states, and visible information.
- Review each gank and tag it as wave-supported, summoner-supported, matchup-supported, or forced.
- Practice pathing with a named first objective: protect lane, punish lane, full clear, invade, or objective setup.
