# Mid Lane Roaming And Priority

Use this note when the transcript discusses mid lane, wave priority, roams, river control, jungle support, side-lane movement, mage control, assassin windows, or mid-game rotations.

Core coaching model:
- Mid lane priority is valuable because mid can move first to river, jungle fights, side lanes, vision, and objectives.
- Roaming is strongest after the wave is pushed or when the player can move without losing too much gold, experience, or tower pressure.
- Mid players should connect champion identity to map movement: control mages often protect space and waves, assassins punish roam windows, and utility mids enable teammates.
- Poor mid play often shows up as moving without wave control, staying mid while a clear roam exists, or taking river fights without lane priority.
- Vision around river and jungle entrances determines whether roams and rotations are safe.

Review questions:
- Did the player have mid priority before moving?
- Was the roam worth the wave, plate, or tempo cost?
- Did the player support the jungler at a timing their wave allowed?
- Was the champion's job control, burst, roam, scaling, or teamfight utility?

Training goals:
- Before roaming, check whether the wave is pushed, held, or crashing into the player.
- Review three river movements and label whether mid priority supported them.
- Track one missed roam and one bad roam per VOD.
- Before objective setup, push mid wave or explain why grouping early is better.
