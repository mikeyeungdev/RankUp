# Mental Process And Autopilot

Use this note when the transcript discusses autopilot, tilt, consistency, decision habits, communication, confidence, overforcing, greed, hesitation, or review process.

Core coaching model:
- Most improvement comes from repeatable decision habits, not trying to remember every possible rule.
- Autopilot often appears as moving without a reason, fighting because teammates fight, recalling randomly, warding late, or chasing after the play is over.
- Tilt and frustration narrow attention. The student may stop tracking waves, timers, cooldowns, or enemy positions.
- A good training plan should define one habit trigger and one success check.
- Confidence should come from process clarity: knowing what to look for before acting.

Review questions:
- What information did the player ignore before this mistake?
- Was the player reacting emotionally, copying teammates, or following a clear plan?
- What simple trigger could prevent this pattern?
- How will the student know they improved after three games?

Training goals:
- Choose one habit trigger for the next block: objective timer, minimap check, wave check, cooldown check, or reset check.
- After each game, write one repeated mistake and one adjustment for the next game.
- Review only one high-leverage theme at a time to avoid overloaded practice.
