# Bot Lane 2v2 Trading

Use this note when the transcript discusses bot lane, ADC support pairing, 2v2 trades, lane pressure, level 2, all-ins, poke, sustain, wave control, or support cooldowns.

Core coaching model:
- Bot lane is a 2v2 lane, so trade quality depends on both champions' cooldowns, range, minion wave, level timing, and follow-up.
- Level 2, level 3, first recall, and item component timings often decide whether bot lane can pressure or must play safely.
- Poke lanes want repeated health advantages, all-in lanes want a committed window, and sustain lanes want to survive and outlast bad trades.
- Bad trades often happen when one partner is out of range, key cooldowns are missing, or the wave makes the trade too expensive.
- Bot pressure should convert into plates, vision, dragon setup, safe recalls, or enemy summoner spells.

Review questions:
- Did both bot-lane players have range and cooldowns to support the trade?
- Was the wave helping or punishing the fight?
- Did the lane respect level 2 and first recall timing?
- Was the trade goal poke, all-in, disengage, crash, or deny farm?

Training goals:
- Review the first three waves and mark level 2 timing.
- Before trading, check partner position and enemy key cooldown.
- Label each bot-lane fight as poke, all-in, disengage, or forced.
- Track whether lane pressure converted into recall, plates, vision, or dragon setup.
