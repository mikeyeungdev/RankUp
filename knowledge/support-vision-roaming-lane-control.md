# Support Vision Roaming And Lane Control

Use this note when the transcript discusses support, warding, sweepers, roam timers, lane pressure, engage, peel, bot lane trading, objective setup, or vision denial.

Core coaching model:
- Support impact comes from creating lane pressure, protecting the carry, controlling vision, and moving at timings that do not sacrifice bot lane unnecessarily.
- Roams are strongest after wave crashes, recalls, deaths, or lane states where the ADC can safely collect the wave.
- Vision should serve the next play: lane safety, river control, objective setup, flank prevention, or jungle tracking.
- Engage supports should track whether allies can follow. Enchanters and peel supports should track which enemy threat must be denied.
- Poor support decisions often come from warding without a purpose, roaming at a bad wave timing, or engaging without ally position.

Review questions:
- Did the support roam from a wave state that protected the ADC?
- What play did each ward or sweeper enable?
- Was the support's job engage, peel, poke, sustain, vision control, or roam pressure?
- Did the bot lane trade with cooldown, level, wave, or matchup advantage?

Training goals:
- Review every roam and label the wave state before leaving lane.
- Before warding, name the next play the ward supports.
- Track whether engages had allied follow-up and enemy escape tools accounted for.
- Before objective spawn, reset early enough to place and deny vision safely.
