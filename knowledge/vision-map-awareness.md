# Vision And Map Awareness

Research basis:
- League of Legends Wiki ward overview: https://wiki.leagueoflegends.com/en-us/Stealth_Ward_%28Item%29
- MMRadar macro guide: https://mmradar.gg/blog/league-of-legends-macro-guide
- Hyse coaching role focus areas: https://hyse.gg/

Use this note when the transcript discusses wards, control wards, sweepers, fog of war, jungle tracking, river control, flanks, or face-checking.

Core coaching model:
- Vision turns uncertain movement into informed decisions.
- Wards are strongest when tied to the next expected play: objective setup, lane pressure, jungle tracking, flank prevention, or safe reset.
- Clearing enemy vision is as important as placing allied vision because it controls what the enemy can safely start.
- Map awareness is a repeatable habit: track missing enemies, jungle pathing, wave states, and objective timers.
- Face-checking is often a planning error before it is a mechanics error.

Review questions:
- What decision did this ward support?
- Did the player ward before the play or after danger already arrived?
- Did the player respect missing enemies and fog of war?
- Was vision placed around the next objective, the next wave, or a random area?

Training goals:
- Before crossing river or entering jungle, identify which enemies are visible and which are missing.
- On every reset, decide whether the next control ward supports lane safety, objective setup, or flank denial.
- In VOD review, tag every death from fog as missing info, ignored info, or forced face-check.
