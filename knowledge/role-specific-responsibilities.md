# Role-Specific Responsibilities

Use this note when the transcript discusses top, jungle, mid, ADC, support, roaming, side lane, engage, peel, farming, or role job.

Core coaching model:
- Top lane often revolves around wave control, matchup discipline, Teleport timing, side-lane pressure, and knowing when to group.
- Jungle often revolves around pathing, tracking, tempo, objective setup, and matching ganks to lane states.
- Mid lane often revolves around wave priority, roam timing, jungle support, vision around river, and damage or control in fights.
- ADC often revolves around farm consistency, spacing, threat tracking, item spikes, front-to-back damage, and survival without losing output.
- Support often revolves around lane pressure, vision control, roam timing, engage or peel decisions, and objective setup.
- Role responsibility changes by champion and team composition. Do not force generic role advice if the transcript gives a different job.

Review questions:
- What was the player's role-specific job at this stage of the game?
- Did the player leave lane or group at a timing their role could support?
- Did the player use their role's pressure to help the map?
- Was the mistake role-specific or a general macro issue?

Training goals:
- Write one role job for lane phase, mid game, and teamfights.
- Review three role decisions and label them as lane, map, objective, or fight responsibility.
- Track one role-specific habit across the next three games.
