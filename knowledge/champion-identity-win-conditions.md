# Champion Identity And Win Conditions

Use this note when the transcript discusses champion role, matchup identity, scaling, lane plan, team composition, carry threat, snowballing, or win condition.

Core coaching model:
- Champion identity defines what the player is trying to do before the game becomes chaotic.
- A champion may want to scale, bully lane, roam, dive, peel, split push, front-to-back teamfight, pick, siege, or force skirmishes.
- A win condition is the practical route to winning this specific game, not a generic champion description.
- Good coaching connects build, lane behavior, map movement, and teamfight job to the champion's identity.
- Many mistakes come from identity mismatch: a scaling champion forcing early fights, an engage champion hesitating forever, or a split pusher grouping without a reason.

Review questions:
- What was this champion's job in lane, mid game, and teamfights?
- Did the player's choices match the champion's strongest timing?
- Which ally or enemy champion changed the correct plan?
- Was the player playing toward their win condition or copying a generic pattern?

Training goals:
- Before queueing, write one sentence for the champion's lane plan and one sentence for the teamfight job.
- In review, tag each major mistake as identity mismatch, timing mismatch, or execution error.
- Track whether item choices and rune choices supported the stated game plan.
